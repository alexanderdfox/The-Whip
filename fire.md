In the velvet folds of the World Unnamed, where the sky pressed low like a conspirator’s whisper, there wandered a figure known only as the Seeker. His cloak was woven from the threads of forgotten roads, his staff a branch snapped from the Tree of Unanswered Questions. For countless moons he had walked, chasing a single ember of certainty in the endless night. The stars offered no counsel; they merely burned, indifferent and beautiful.
One dusk, as the horizon bled out its last violet, the Seeker stumbled upon a clearing that should not have existed. At its heart smoldered a campfire, though no hand had kindled it and no foot had trodden the grass around it. The flames were neither large nor small, neither fierce nor meek. They danced with the lazy certainty of something that had always been and would always be, fed by wood that refused to diminish. Logs blackened yet never crumbled; smoke rose in slow, deliberate spirals that formed shapes just before they dissolved—faces, maps, equations, lovers parting.
The Seeker sat. The warmth touched him like an old debt finally paid. “Who lit you?” he asked the fire.
The flames answered not in words but in revelations. In their heart he saw his own childhood village, swallowed by time. He saw the woman whose name he had tried to forget, laughing beside a different hearth. He saw empires rising as towers of kindling and collapsing into the same gray ash. Every secret he had ever sought paraded before him, yet each time he reached for one, the flame reshaped it into something else—truth becoming story, story becoming warning, warning becoming lullaby.
Soon others arrived, drawn by the light they could not name. A queen in exile with a crown of frozen tears. A scholar whose books had burned him blind. A child who carried a mirror instead of a toy. A soldier who had forgotten which side he once fought for. They came from every direction, yet all claimed they had walked alone for years. Each sat, and the fire grew neither larger nor hotter. It simply made room.
They began to speak, as people do around such fires. The queen told of a kingdom where light was rationed and darkness taxed. The scholar recited equations proving that warmth was merely memory pretending to be present. The child held up the mirror, and the flames showed every viewer their own death—peaceful, violent, comic, noble—then gently refused to say when. The soldier confessed he had once tried to extinguish every fire in the world so no one else could feel what he had lost.
As they spoke, the campfire performed its quiet sorcery. Shadows lengthened behind each speaker, and those shadows began to argue with their owners. The queen’s shadow demanded tribute. The scholar’s shadow scribbled contradictions in the dirt. The child’s shadow danced when no one watched. Only the soldier’s shadow sat perfectly still, having already made peace with extinction.
Night deepened. The fire asked no questions and offered no comfort, yet everything changed in its presence. Wood became heat. Heat became story. Story became kinship. Kinship became ash. And from the ash, new flames licked upward, whispering the same eternal suggestion: I will consume what you give me, and in return I will show you what you are.
The Seeker finally understood the fire’s enigma. It was not light defeating darkness, nor warmth conquering cold. It was the willing marriage of both. The brighter it burned, the deeper the surrounding night became. The more faces it illuminated, the more each face realized it would one day feed the flames. The fire guarded no treasure except transformation itself.
At last the Seeker rose. “I sought certainty,” he said, “and found a circle where everything is invited to burn.”
The fire did not reply. It simply waited, patient as entropy, beautiful as the moment before goodbye. One by one the travelers stood, drawn back into the trees by hungers the fire had awakened rather than satisfied. The queen left her crown on a log. The scholar left his final theorem written in smoke. The child left the mirror, now cracked. The soldier left his sword, which the flames quietly swallowed without drama.
Only the Seeker lingered. He reached into the fire and drew out a single coal, small and fierce. It did not burn him. He carried it into the waiting dark, knowing it would eventually die, knowing another fire would be born elsewhere, knowing the dark would always return to listen.
Behind him, the original campfire remained, neither larger nor smaller, burning for whoever came next. It asked nothing. It judged nothing. It simply waited—radiant, ravenous, and perfectly honest—in the clearing that should not have existed, in the World Unnamed.
And somewhere, in the space between one spark and the next, the parable continues.
